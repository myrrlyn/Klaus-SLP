This is the website explaining and facilitating the Senior Leadership Practicum of Nicholas Frederick Payne. For one week, I led a group of 58 Scouts on Mackinac Island, a state park in Michigan. This will count towards my ability to graduate from Culver Military Academy, in the Company C class of 2018. For more information, follow the links provided on the left hand side of the page.

Goals, Objectives, and Activities

This is a review of the goal-setting process.

  • Goal: The goal of my project is to provide consistent and capable service to Troop 623 and Mackinac Island State park while mentoring and teaching the young Scouts.
  • Measurable Outcome:
    1. Tourists are satisfied, no complaints from tourists or GSI.
    2. Tasks are completed - 3 service projects and punctual execution of daily tasks.
    3. Island is improved - police line Friday night and Saturday morning.
    4. Successful grading on midweek and final evaluation.
  • Objectives:

    1. Represent the Boy Scouts and the Governor's Honor Guard with pride and excellence in appearance:
      • Marching form - rehearsed in two practices and competitively evaluated in the Thursday night marching competition.
      • Uniform quality - ensured through routine inspections by Patrol Leaders and Assistant Patrol Leaders in formation. Junior Leadership debriefs will communicate uniform standards clearly.
      • Punctuality - each patrol, under the leadership of the Patrol Leader or Assistant Patrol Leader, will arrive at flag and duty posts on time or early to maintain a professional appearance and troop morale.

    2. Provide excellence in service:
      • Duty shifts in fort and downtown - overseen by the ASPL and myself each day, the conduct of Scouts will be a key point at all times.
      • Off-duty service projects - the troop will clean and collect garbage for the Fort as well as taking on at least three service projects during the week.
      • Flag retirement - Friday night the Troop will host a retirement ceremony and burn the flags no longer fit for use, donated in the St. Joseph region.

    3. Display proper function and behavior as a Troop:
      • Communication - the Troop will be kept informed and updated through proper information dissemination at each meal's announcements and Taps Report.
      • Behavior - behavior will be moderated by the Junior Leadership and controlled through Extra Duty and the awarding of the Governor's Mansion duty shift.
      • Off duty activities - euchre, horseshoe, and Magic the Gathering tournaments will be set up in the barracks. In addition, behavior in town while off duty will be stressed and evaluated by youth leadership.
  • Activities:
    1. Drill Competition
    2. Flag Presentation and Retreat
    3. Off-duty activities (going downtown, touring the island)
    4. Retirement of the Colors
    5. Inspiration Point
